Atelier Re-Commence

Repair. Relate. Re-imagine.
A circular economy makerspace and social enterprise where communities extend the life of objects, learn practical skills, and build lasting relationships.
The Problem

Repair is an act of care that is disappearing.

To repair something is to declare it worth keeping. It is a relationship, a commitment, a form of memory. But fast fashion and throwaway culture have severed that connection. Repair skills are vanishing. The people who still carry them, often older or overlooked, have no platform to pass them on. And the objects that hold our stories are ending up in landfill instead of in someone's hands.

The Vision

A place where repair becomes culture.

Atelier Re-Commence is a community-rooted makerspace and social enterprise. It is a blend of a welcoming café, hands-on workshops, a living textile archive, and a flexible kitchen and dining space, all under one roof and one mission: integrating repair and reuse into everyday culture.

"What if the next big thing was actually the small things?"

The Four Hubs

One atelier, four entry points.

Hub 01
Beans & Seeds Café
A cozy anchor space for gathering, conversation, and discovering circular living. Low-waste menu sourced from local vendors, community bulletin, and Repair & Sip hours.
Hub 02
The Makerspace
Hands-on repair clinics, tool library, skill certifications. Sliding-scale memberships. Partnerships with schools, libraries, and repair professionals.
Hub 03
Garment Stories
A living textile archive preserving the memories of material and culture. Community donations, story intake days, and exhibitions connecting repair to identity and memory.
Hub 04 · working title
The Kitchen + Dining Space
A shared kitchen and dining area available to local caterers, chefs, and community groups. Supplements the café and hosts heritage kitchen nights and community gatherings.
Who We Serve

Built around people and purpose.

Atelier Re-Commence is designed for people who carry the culture within them and who want to share their experience with others.
Neighbors
People who want affordable repair, practical skills, and a sense of belonging in their own neighborhood.
Makers & Students
Artists, designers, and students who need tools, space, mentorship, and access to materials they cannot find elsewhere.
Local Organizations
Schools, nonprofits, and businesses looking for circular solutions, kitchen and dining access, and community programming.
Keepers
People of all ages who carry repair knowledge, craft traditions, and lived experience and who are ready to pass it on.
Business Model

Sustainable by design.

Revenue streams are diversified across the four hubs, reducing dependency on any single source while keeping the mission central.
Revenue Stream 01
Café + Retail
Daily foot traffic, low-waste menu, circular merchandise, and upcycled goods sold through the space.
Revenue Stream 02
Memberships + Classes
Sliding-scale Makerspace memberships, skill workshops, and certifications. Sponsored seats for low-income members.
Revenue Stream 03
Kitchen + Dining Rentals
Supplementary income from local caterers, chefs, and community groups renting the kitchen and dining space for events and heritage kitchen nights.
Revenue Stream 04
Grants + Partnerships
ESS sector funding, municipal circular economy grants, and anchor institution partnerships in each city.
Market & Locations

The Atelier concept is designed to travel. Each city brings its own community character while the four-hub model remains consistent.
